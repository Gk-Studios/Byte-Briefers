First, What are they?

Well, Large Language Models, or LLMs, are very complicated and it is very hard to explain, so we are going to explain a crude version of it. LLMs are a type of AI that is designed to understand, generate, and process our language or daily speech. LLMs mainly consist of billions to trillions of parameters. These parameters are trained by analyzing millions of batchs of text and human interactions to try to be able to predict a response. They are basically an input and ouput algorithim, but these simple algorithims struggle with complex inputs or or a lot of variables. So this is where Neural Networks come in, these networks can compute extremely complex situations. They simply consist of multiple layers of connected processors or "neurons" that handle small inputs at a time. They are basically lots of simple linear algorithims combined to predict an arbitrary or nonlinear outcome. Advanced models like ChatGPT have around almost 176 billion "neurons". History of the LLM

LLMs have come a long way. From their beginnings as simple algirithoms, to these very powerful and complicated machines. Lets look at the very beginning. Large Language Models (LLMs) first began in 1966 with ELIZA, a plain chatbot that mimicked conversations, using predefined rules but didn't have true understanding. From 1974-1980, scientists dealed with 2 main problems: the programs processed extremely slow, and it couldn't hold big batchs of data. In the 1980, IBM created a small neural network model that tried to predict the next word in a sentence after "analyzing" it using a "dictionary". The "dictionary" helped the algirithom find how often a word is typically used in a conversation/text. Later more advanced GPUs (Graphic Processing Unit) were introduced allowing models to be faster, stronger , and more reliable. In the 1990s Deep Machine Learning was introduced along with the World Wide Web which helped create even more sophisticated machines. The World Wide Web allowed LLMs to access massive amounts of information. GPUs helped accelerate neural networks since they helped them quickly proccess images or batchs of text. Deep Machine Learning allowed neural nerworks to be pre-trained on large sets of data, but it only got popular in the 2010s. Neural networks that were trained with Deep Machine Learning eventually became LLMs or Large Language Models later. In 2014, the concept of Generative Adversarial Neural Network was introduced where 2 neural networks genrate a photo and the other looked at it and would find flaws and give feedback. This continued exchanging between them until one of the neural networks generated a image that the other neural network couldn't detect any flaws. This is important since it can show how these neural networks are able to replicate or mimic real life objects or animals through an image. Finally in 2022 OpenAI released ChatGPT, a chatbot that could accurately communicate in normal and human-like speech. Uses of LLMs

There are many uses of LLMs whether personal or professional, we can agree that LLMs have become an important part of some peoples everyday life. Here are some main ways some people use LLMs:
1. Data Analysis: These are typically used for Spoken Data Analysis because they can turn an long recording for example a few hours into summaries or insights really quickly. This can help save time and boost productivity in the professional world since now, they dont have to listen to the whole recording.
2. Content Creation: There are many people use LLMs for Content Creation for reasons like inspiration, helping to complete araticles, reports , and etc. LLMs help writers, marketers, and more.
3. Language translation and localization: LLMs can help companys or businesses achieve a wider audience. This can be used in appliances like websites, articles, and more. LLMs can accurately and effectively translate things quickly so people can save time and energy.
4. Education and Training: LLMs can help people like teachers help create a personalized education for others. LLMs can for example create questions, help to tutor, or create a learning level fit for the student.
5. Cybersecurity: LLMs can help with cybersecurity by analyzing massive amounts of security data to spot an anomanly or pattern that could be missed by human analysts. LLMs can sometimes be more reliable and effective than human analysts which is why lots of businesses use them.
